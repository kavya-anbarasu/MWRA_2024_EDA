Kavya's analysis

Seasonal stratification is the clearest 2024 signal.

The profiles start close to mixed in winter and early spring, separate sharply by June, stay stratified through late summer, then relax again by October. That seasonal structure is visible in temperature, oxygen, and plankton timing.

Quick read

Water column pattern

February through April profiles are nearly mixed: the average surface-minus-deep temperature difference stays under 1 C. By May the profile opens up, and the strongest separation appears from June through August. Cape Cod Bay and several farfield stations show the largest individual gaps, while nearfield stations also develop strong late-summer separation.

Salinity gradients are much smaller than the temperature gradients, generally around 0.1 to 1.1 PSU by month. That makes the 2024 stratification pattern read mostly as a seasonal heating signal rather than a strong salinity-front story.

Chemistry and biology

Oxygen declines late in the season

Deep dissolved oxygen averages are above 10 mg/L in February and March, then trend downward through summer and early fall, reaching about 7.2 mg/L in October. The minimum probe value in this extract is 5.37 mg/L at N01 near 30 m on Oct 17. That points to late-season oxygen drawdown under stratification, but not a broad severe low-oxygen event in the visible records.

The lab and plankton data add useful context: N21 stands out with the highest mean total dissolved nitrogen among sampled stations, and the plankton records are dominated by small microflagellates and centric diatoms on the phytoplankton side, with copepods making up the largest zooplankton group.
